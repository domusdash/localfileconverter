How to Convert HEIC to JPG Locally

Apple devices (iPhones and iPads) save photos in High Efficiency Image Container (HEIC) format by default to save storage space. However, many websites, resumes, and web forms do not accept HEIC files. To convert them:

  1. Drag and drop your .heic files into the upload box above.
  2. Select your target format (such as JPEG, PNG, or WebP).
  3. Adjust the quality slider (80% quality balances compression with visual clarity).
  4. Click Convert All to run the local WebAssembly converter. Download individual files or grab the compiled ZIP file.

Convert PDF Pages to High-Resolution JPG Images

Need to extract specific pages of a presentation, contract, or ebook? Rather than taking screenshots, our local PDF rendering engine lets you convert pages into print-quality image files:

  • 72 DPI (Web): Perfect for low-file-size layouts, emails, or drafts.

  • 300 DPI (High Print): Super-crisp vector-like rendering suitable for printing or graphic design work.
  • Custom Page Ranges: Extract only what you need. Type page ranges like 1-3, 5 to skip unnecessary pages and minimize download size.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are my files uploaded to a server?

No. Blueprint Converter is designed as a local-first utility. All PDF, HEIC, and image calculations are performed entirely in your browser using JavaScript and WebAssembly. Your files never leave your computer, making it 100% safe for sensitive and private documents.

Does this app work without an internet connection?

Yes. Once you load the page once, all conversion libraries are cached in your browser. You can disconnect from the internet and continue converting files completely offline.

Is there a file size limit?

No. Unlike cloud converters that limit you to 10MB or 20MB files, Blueprint Converter processes files on your local CPU. You can load multi-gigabyte files; the only limit is your system's available memory.

Why is local conversion faster?

Standard online converters require you to upload your files to their server, wait in a queue, let the server convert the file, and then download the result. With local conversion, we bypass the upload and download steps completely. Processing starts instantly, saving you time and bandwidth.
